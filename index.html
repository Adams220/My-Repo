Meditation

What Is Meditation?

Meditation is a practice of using the mind and body to focus on a particular object. It is usually done individually, in a seated position and with eyes closed.
In our society today, meditation is an extremely common practice and one that has helped millions of people find inner peace in their lives.
Meditation is also practiced in Hinduism, Christianity and Islam. However, it is important to note that a person does not need to be religious to meditate.

History of Meditation

Although it is believed that meditation was originated in ancient India decades ago, it involves Vedantism which is a Hindu tradition, other cultures and religions across the world also practiced a form of meditation. From 3500 - 5000 BCE, cave paintings were found in India with images of people in meditative postures with their eyes closed.

In some of the oldest written records from around 1500 BCE in India, the practice of Dhyāna or Jhāna is referenced as the training of the mind, often translated as meditation. Many of these records come from the Hindu traditions of Vedantism and discuss the various meditation practices across ancient India.

Benefits of Meditation

Depending on the type of meditation you choose, you can meditate to relax, reduce anxiety and stress. meditation can help to improve your health and habits.

  1. Meditation improves confidence and positive lifestyle. Regular meditation reduces depression and negative thoughts.
  2. Meditation helps you develop a better understanding of your inner self. It reduces the feeling of loneliness and brings happiness.
  3. Meditation can also help prevent memory loss. Meditation improves performance on neuropsychological testing. Regular meditation improves mental clarity and are more lucid and functional.
  4. Pain is linked to the mind and can be elevated when you are under stress. Meditation can help reduce the perception of pain in the brain.
    This leads to an improved quality of life and a better mood.
  5. Meditation improves working memory and fluid intelligence.

How to Meditate

Although there are many forms of meditation and ways to practice, the type of meditation that is best for you is the one you will enjoy doing. If you enjoy it, you’re more likely to develop a consistent practice.

  • Find a calm quiet environment/place and avoid any distractions.
  • Set a time limit if you are a beginner, you might want to stick
    to shorter sessions for about 10 minutes length each day.
  • Sit in a chair, cushion or on the floor with your legs crossed.
    You should sit upright and do not sit in a drooping posture
  • Close your eyes and gently gaze downwards.
  • Gently breathe in through your nose for some few seconds and breathe out,
    find your own rhythm, find your own pace.
  • Focus on a point. Notice when your mind wanders, breathe in, out gently and go back to that focus point.
Side effects of Meditation

During meditation your mind wanders as you meditate. This can lead to thoughts that are uncomfortable, distressing and anxiety.

After meditation, you can be vulnerable to outside influences. Avoiding making decision or taking on important things immediately after meditating.

Meditation can cause anxiety attacks in people because it tends to bring back all kinds of memories. Negative emotions may erupt during a meditation practice

It can also induce feelings of negativity. This in turn can sometimes cause feelings of dissociation and an unwillingness to connect with other people.

Meditation helps you develop an understanding with yourself, it may change the way you see others and make it difficult for you to bond with others.

Yoga and Meditation

Dhyāna or Jhāna is a form of meditation and also part of the eight steps of yoga but there is a difference between yoga and meditation.
Yoga is an exercise that involves physical postures to achieve physical and mental health.

Yoga is mostly done in a class with an instructor. Although you can practice on your own, people prefer to practice in a class because the atmosphere
is inspiring and motivating.
